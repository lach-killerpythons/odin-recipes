Cauliflower Blue Cheese Soup - Hearty French Soup Recipe



I have to admit, I was never the biggest fan of blue cheese until I dipped a chicken wing in some blue cheese sauce!

I was at my local supermarket, and something jumped out at me in the cheese section - half price blue cheese

I quickly remembered I had a leek, home-made chicken stock and a big old cauliflower and milk at home that needed to be used up

In that moment I had an incredible idea for dinner!

I was certainly not the first person to think of this - but if you looking for a rich French style soup on a cold night would highly recommend!


Ingredients

  • 1 large leek (or 1 white onion) - cut into small pieces
  • [OPTIONAL] Small chunk of speck (or bacon) diced
  • Knob of butter
  • Half a head of cauliflower
  • Chicken stock
  • Milk
  • Cooking cream
  • Small portion of blue cheese
  • Olive oil (for cooking + finishing)
  • Parsley (for garnish)
  • Rustic bread (to serve with)
>

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 200*C / 392*F
  2. Cut up cauliflower into large pieces and put into a large baking dish.
  3. Season cauliflower with olive oil + salt and roast for 20-25 mins in oven till chared
  4. In a large pot, render down speck (bacon) until crispy + fat renders
  5. Add the leek to the pot with some butter and sweat on a reduced heat
  6. Add chicken stock when this starts to dry out and scrape the fond from the bottom
  7. Add the roasted cauliflower + milk + blue cheese to the pot - slightly submerging the cauliflower + cheese
  8. Give the cheese time to melt in the boiling liquid and the cauliflower to fall apart
  9. When the cheese + milk form a homogenous mixture use an immersion blender to blitz the cauliflower/leek/pork
  10. Based on how creamy your current mixture is, add a dolop of cream using your intuition
  11. Once everything is well combined - serve in a bowl topped with diced parsley, olive oil and toasted bread
